A. Proposal Summary (As Claimed)

The proposal claims that cosmic acceleration can arise from thermodynamic properties assigned to a cosmological horizon, without introducing new matter fields or modifying the gravitational action. By associating entropy and temperature with the Hubble (or apparent) horizon, an effective entropic force is argued to modify cosmological dynamics and produce accelerated expansion.

B. Claimed Mechanism (As Claimed)

Accelerated expansion arises from horizon thermodynamics. Entropy and temperature are assigned to a cosmological horizon, and an entropic force derived from these quantities is argued to contribute an additional term to the cosmological acceleration equation.

Explicit authorial claims:

No inflaton field is introduced

No new propagating degrees of freedom are added

Standard cosmological matter and radiation remain present

Interpretive note (CEDA translation only):
The proposal treats the horizon as the origin of an effective force/pressure influencing expansion, without promoting the horizon to a dynamical field in the action.
